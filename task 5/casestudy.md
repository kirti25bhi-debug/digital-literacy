Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. 
This is commonly done through emails, SMS, or fake websites.
A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives a message that appears to be from a legitimate organization, such as a bank or online service. 
The message often creates urgency, for example, stating that the account will be blocked unless immediate action is taken. It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the real one. 
When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures this information. In some cases, the attacker may also request an OTP to complete unauthorized transactions.

College students are frequent targets due to their active online presence and use of digital payment platforms. 
The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may also face emotional stress and loss of trust in online systems.

This case highlights the importance of verifying sources, avoiding suspicious links, and being cautious while sharing personal information online.
